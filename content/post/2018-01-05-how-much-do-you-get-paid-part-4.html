Salaries by academy category

In England, the Football Association (FA) sets the academy status of all football clubs with academy programs, as part of their Elite Player Performance Plan. Club academies are audited and given a status of Category 1 to 4, with 1 being the highest tier possible. Meanwhile, in Scotland, a new academy structure has been recently introduced with three levels: Elite (the highest tier), Progressive (the middle tier), and Performance (the lowest tier). In both countries, different levels of grant funding are awarded based on their academy category.

For context (and my own understanding), I’ve patched together a range of news sources to collate the following information about category-specific funding:

  • In England, grants are awarded to academies based on their category status and are scaled to a percentage of their operational budget, according to this source. Therefore, the absolute values cited below are ‘ball-park’ figures.
  • Category 1 academies received a minimum £775,000 per year as a grant from the Premier League’s youth development fund in 2013, according to an article by Wayne Veysey for Goal.com.
  • According to the Veysey article, Category 2 academies could expect a minimum of £480,000 in funding in 2013. In a news item from 2016 by Laura Hunter for the Coventry City FC website, their Category 2 academy received ~£500,000 in funding that year.
  • The Veysey article states that Category 3 academies received a minimum of £210,000 in funding in 2013.
  • Category 4 academies received a minimum of £100,000 in funding in 2013, as per Veysey.
  • I couldn’t find any public information on funding for Scottish academies, which might be due to the fact that the new academy structure was finalised recently, in November 2017. Do you know of any reliable information sources on this topic? Send me links via Twitter!

Knowing this, it makes sense to investigate whether academy category (read: academy budget) affects the salaries of performance support staff in the UK.

Side note: Two respondents working in Scotland indicated that their category status was ‘Development’. # Subset of data frame to exclude academyCategory == NA
soccerSalariesData4 <- soccerSalariesData %>%
    filter(!is.na(academyCategory))

# Set factor levels
soccerSalariesData4$academyCategory <- factor(
    soccerSalariesData4$academyCategory,
    levels = c("1", "2", "3", "SFA Elite", "Development"))

# Create object to store axis labels with line breaks
labelsAcademyCategory <- c(
    "England\nCategory 1", "England\nCategory 2", "England\nCategory 3",
    "Scotland\nElite", "Scotland\n'Development'")

# Create a list to store common theme options for use across plots
plotFeatures <- theme(
    plot.title = element_text(size = 16),
    plot.subtitle = element_text(size = 14),
    axis.title.x = element_blank(),
  axis.text.x = element_text(size = 11),
    axis.title.y = element_text(size = 16),
    axis.text.y = element_text(size = 11),
    legend.position = "none")

p <- ggplot(soccerSalariesData4, aes(x = academyCategory, y = salaryGBP))
p <- p + geom_boxplot(outlier.shape = NA, coef = 0)
p <- p + geom_point(alpha = 1/4, size = 4, aes(colour = academyCategory))
p <- p + scale_y_continuous(
    limits = c(0, 50000),
    breaks = seq(0, 50000, by = 10000))
p <- p + scale_x_discrete(labels = labelsAcademyCategory)
p <- p + labs(
    title = "UK soccer salaries of support staff: Academy staff only",
    subtitle = "By academy category",
    y = "Salary (GBP)")
p <- p + plotFeatures
salaryByAcademyCategory <- p

Let’s also tabulate median salaries by academy category:

# Calculate median salaries by academy category
medianSalariesByAcademyCategory <- soccerSalariesData4 %>%
    group_by(academyCategory) %>%
    summarise(median = median(salaryGBP, na.rm = TRUE))

# Rename values in $academyCategory for aesthetic purposes
medianSalariesByAcademyCategory$academyCategory <- c(
    "England Category 1", "England Category 2", "England Category 3",
    "Scotland Elite", "Scotland 'Development'")

# Rename variables for aesthetic purposes
colnames(medianSalariesByAcademyCategory) <- c(
    "Academy category status", "Median salary (GBP, £)")
